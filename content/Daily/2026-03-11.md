#### Variational Monte Carlo
- Definition : $|\psi_\theta\rangle = \sum_{\vec{x}}\psi_\theta(\vec{x})|\vec{x}\rangle$, so variational manifold is the set $\mathcal{M}=\{|\psi_\theta\rangle|\theta\in\mathbb{R}^P\}$.
- Evaluation of Observable : $\braket{Q}_{\psi_\theta}=\frac{\sum_{\vec{x}}\braket{\psi_\theta|\vec{x}}\braket{\vec{x}|Q|\psi_\theta}}{\sum_{\vec{x}'}\braket{\psi_\theta|\vec{x}'}\braket{\vec{x}'|\psi_\theta}}=\sum_{\vec{x}}p_\theta(\vec{x})Q_L(\vec{x})$, CLT used when we evalute as approximated $\frac{1}{M}\sum_i Q_L(\vec{x}_i)$ to avoid the exponential number of basis vector $\vec{x}$.
- Markov-chain Monte Carlo samplers : Accepted/Rejected new sample accord to the Metropolis-Hastings probability : $p_{accept}=min(1,p(\vec{x}')/p(\vec{x}))$. And generate new $\vec{x}'$ by local Metropolis sampler (relying on small local changes)/cluster update scheme (e.g. Wolff algorithm for the Ising model)/...
- Autoregressive sampling : Beyond MCMC, autoregressive sampling is used by considering conditional probability decomposition and iterating (1. sample $\vec{x}_i\sim p(\vec{x}_i|\vec{x}_{<i}$ and concatenate to $\vec{x}_{<i}$; 2. use sample to define p($\vec{x}_{i+1}|\vec{x}_{<i+1})$). This can be exact, parallel-friendly and less auto-correlation but require being able to
efficiently and exactly sample each conditional p (i.e. $\vec{x}_i$ have to be shosen to be low dimensional).
- Stochastic optimization : Instead of using relevant gradient $g=\nabla_\theta E(\theta)$, we use evolution trick to filer out ground state s.t. $|\psi_{\theta +\delta \theta}\rangle\propto e^{-\delta\tau H}|\psi_\theta\rangle$. Starting from the imaginary time evolution $|\psi_{\theta+\delta\tau}\rangle \propto e^{-\delta\tau H}|\psi_{\theta}\rangle$, a first-order expansion in $\delta\tau$ and $\delta\theta$ leads to the linear equation $\sum_{\mu} \mathcal{O}_{\mu} \dot{\theta}^{\mu} |\psi_{\theta}\rangle = -H |\psi_{\theta}\rangle$ where $\mathcal{O}_{\mu} = \partial_{\theta^\mu} \ln \psi_{\theta}$; minimizing the residual $||\sum_{\mu} \mathcal{O}_{\mu} \dot{\theta}^{\mu} + H||$ by projecting onto the variational manifold tangent space $\langle\psi_{\theta}|\mathcal{O}_{\nu}^{\dagger}$ yields the update rule $S \dot{\theta} = -g$ , where the quantum geometric tensor $S_{\mu\nu} = 2 \text{Re}\{\langle \mathcal{O}_{\mu}^{\dagger}\mathcal{O}_{\nu} \rangle - \langle \mathcal{O}_{\mu}^{\dagger} \rangle \langle \mathcal{O}_{\nu} \rangle\}$ acts as the metric and the energy gradient $g_{\mu} = 2 \text{Re}\{\langle \mathcal{O}_{\mu}^{\dagger}H \rangle - \langle \mathcal{O}_{\mu}^{\dagger} \rangle \langle H \rangle\}$ represents the force.
